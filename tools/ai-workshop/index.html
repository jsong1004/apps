Training That Produces Real Results

The AI training market is crowded with courses that promise transformation and deliver slide decks. The AI Workshop is different in one fundamental way: every module ends with a working automation that you have built and deployed yourself. Not a demonstration, not a screenshot, not a walkthrough — a live system that you can use in your actual work starting the day after you complete the module.

The curriculum is designed for the three types of business professionals who want to build AI automation but aren't sure where to start. The first group is operations managers and process owners who want to automate the repetitive tasks that consume their teams. The second is entrepreneurs and founders who want to build AI-powered products without hiring developers. The third is consultants and advisors who want to deliver AI capabilities to their clients rather than just recommending tools.

What you'll build in the AI Workshop depends on your context, but modules are structured around common business automation use cases: automated email triage and response drafting, AI-powered data analysis and reporting, document processing and information extraction, research automation and synthesis, and customer support automation. Each module uses real tools — Google Gemini, Cursor IDE, and cloud deployment via Google Cloud Run — so the skills transfer directly to your work environment.

Jaehee Song's teaching approach, refined over years of lecturing at DGIST (Korea's premier science and technology university), Inha University, and Goorm, is built around the principle that the best way to learn a new capability is to build something real with it immediately. This is the same vibe coding philosophy behind the Build with AI series — learn by doing, not by studying.

Compared to generic AI courses, the AI Workshop is narrowly focused on a specific outcome: business automation that you can deploy and measure. It doesn't cover AI theory, machine learning fundamentals, or the history of artificial intelligence. It covers exactly what you need to know to build, deploy, and maintain AI-powered automations in a business context — and nothing more. This focus is what allows participants with no coding background to build working systems in their first session.
