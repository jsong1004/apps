InNews

AI-Powered Newsletter Platform

InNews is a multi-tenant newsletter platform that gives teams everything they need to create, schedule, and analyze email newsletters — with a rich text editor, recipient management, role-based access controls, and deep analytics built in from day one.

Features

Rich Text Editor

Full WYSIWYG editor for professional newsletters without HTML knowledge. Format text, embed images, add links, and preview your newsletter exactly as subscribers will see it.

Scheduling & Automation

Schedule newsletters days or weeks ahead; set recurring sends; automate welcome sequences for new subscribers so every new sign-up receives the right message at the right time.

Recipient Management

Segment your audience, manage subscriptions, import lists, and track bounces and unsubscribes in real time. Keep your list healthy and your deliverability high.

Analytics Dashboard

Open rates, click-through rates, device breakdown, and geographic distribution — all in one dashboard. Know exactly how each newsletter performs and what content resonates.

Role-Based Access

Writers, editors, and admins each have appropriate permissions. No more shared passwords or accidental sends — every action is tied to an authenticated user with the right role.

Multi-Tenant Architecture

Each team or client gets an isolated workspace. Perfect for agencies managing newsletters for multiple brands — data, settings, and subscribers are always kept separate.

Why InNews?

Most organizations rely on generic email marketing tools that were designed for e-commerce campaigns, not editorial newsletters. The result is clunky editing experiences, limited audience segmentation, and analytics that don't tell the real story of subscriber engagement. InNews was built from scratch with newsletter teams in mind — whether you're running a company newsletter, a client-facing publication, or an internal digest.

The problem with shared email marketing accounts is that everyone has the same permissions. A junior writer shouldn't be able to send to your full list, and a contractor shouldn't have access to subscriber data they don't need. InNews solves this with true role-based access control — writers draft, editors approve, and admins configure. Every action is logged so you always know who did what and when.

For agencies managing newsletters on behalf of multiple clients, the multi-tenant architecture is the critical differentiator. Each client workspace is fully isolated — separate subscriber lists, separate analytics, separate branding, separate billing. There's no risk of cross-contamination between client accounts, and each client gets a clean interface that feels like it was built just for them.

InNews was originally built by Jaehee Song for Seattle Partners LLC and consulting clients who needed a professional newsletter infrastructure without the cost and complexity of enterprise platforms. Consulting clients in Seattle's startup and corporate ecosystem needed a tool that could handle everything from internal team updates to investor newsletters to customer product announcements — all from a single platform with clear separation between workspaces.

The scheduling and automation features are where InNews saves teams the most time. Set up a welcome sequence once, and every new subscriber gets a thoughtful onboarding experience automatically. Schedule your weekly digest for Friday morning and walk away — InNews handles delivery, tracks opens and clicks, and surfaces the insights you need to improve the next edition.

Analytics in InNews go beyond simple open rates. Geographic distribution shows where your most engaged subscribers are located. Device breakdown tells you whether your subscribers read on mobile (and whether your layout works on small screens). Time-based engagement data shows the optimal send window for your specific audience. These insights compound over time to produce a newsletter that gets smarter with every edition.
