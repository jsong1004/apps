What Is a PRD? What Is a BRD?

Product Requirements Document (PRD)

A Product Requirements Document (PRD) defines what a product must do — its features, user stories, acceptance criteria, and success metrics. It's the primary reference for your engineering, design, and QA teams during development. Without a clear PRD, teams build the wrong features, miss edge cases, and waste sprint cycles on requirements clarification.

A complete PRD typically includes: product overview, target users, problem statement, feature list with user stories, acceptance criteria per feature, out-of-scope items, success metrics, and non-functional requirements (performance, security, accessibility).

Business Requirements Document (BRD)

A Business Requirements Document (BRD) precedes the PRD. It defines the business problem and the outcomes a project must achieve — without specifying the solution. The BRD gets stakeholders aligned on the "why" before the team decides "what" to build. It covers: business objectives, current-state pain points, stakeholders, constraints, and measurable success criteria.

Key difference: The BRD defines the business need (why). The PRD defines the product solution (what). Clearly generates both document types through separate AI-guided workflows.
