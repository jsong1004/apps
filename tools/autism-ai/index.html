Accessible Education Where It Matters Most

Autism spectrum disorder affects approximately 1 in 36 children in the United States, and the families, educators, and healthcare workers who support autistic individuals need access to accurate, current, and understandable information. Despite the prevalence of autism, quality educational resources are often locked behind paywalls, written in clinical language that's inaccessible to non-specialists, or buried in search results behind outdated or misleading content.

Autism AI addresses this gap directly. The platform is built on the belief that accurate, compassionate information about autism should be accessible to every family and educator who needs it — regardless of their income, educational background, or geographic location. By combining AI language capabilities with evidence-based content, the platform can answer specific questions in plain language and adapt its responses to the needs of the person asking.

The AI-guided Q&A feature is particularly valuable for families in the early stages of understanding an autism diagnosis. When a parent receives a diagnosis for their child, they have hundreds of questions and don't know where to start. They want to know: What does this mean for my child's future? How should I talk to their school? What communication strategies work best? What support services are available? The Autism AI platform can address these questions with thoughtful, evidence-based answers that reference specific research and point toward professional resources for follow-up.

Educators represent another critical user group. Teachers and school counselors who have autistic students in their classrooms often receive limited training on autism support strategies. The practical guides in the resource library — organized by setting, age group, and specific support need — give educators actionable strategies they can implement in the classroom immediately.

The platform is designed with accessibility as a core principle — not an afterthought. Content is available in multiple reading levels, visual and auditory presentation options are supported, and the interface is designed to be navigable by users with a wide range of digital literacy. The Korean-American community is also specifically considered in the resource library, with culturally sensitive guidance for Korean families navigating autism diagnosis and support in the US healthcare and education systems.
